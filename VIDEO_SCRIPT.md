# PAWS Demo Video Script (~3 minutes)
# Record voiceover separately or use text-to-speech
# Hashtag: #AmazonNova

---

## [0:00–0:15] HOOK — Landing Page

> It's 2 AM in Nepal. A farmer named Bishnu is sitting in the dark at the edge of his field with a flashlight — because last week, an elephant herd destroyed six months of crops in forty minutes.
>
> 750 million smallholder farmers face this every night. PAWS is the system that lets Bishnu sleep.

---

## [0:15–0:35] WHAT IT IS — Enter Dashboard

> PAWS — Perimeter AI Wildlife Surveillance. Connect any IP camera to a GPU-accelerated detection pipeline backed by Amazon Nova foundation models.
>
> This is the command center. Live camera feeds on the left, real-time pipeline trace in the center, Nova AI analysis on the right. Dark, light, and green themes — because a farmer checking at 2 AM needs low glare.

---

## [0:35–1:00] ADD CAMERA + START INFERENCE

> Let me add a live camera. This is a real elephant enclosure streaming over HLS. I name it, paste the URL, select the type, and hit Add.
>
> Now I press Start Inference. This sends the stream to a Modal.com serverless T4 GPU running YOLO-World — an open-vocabulary object detector that watches for region-specific species. For an Asian farm: elephants, tigers, leopards, bears. For Kenya: lions, hyenas, crocodiles. Zero retraining — just text prompts.

---

## [1:00–1:30] LIVE DETECTION — Bounding Boxes + Pipeline

> Watch the pipeline terminal. Every step publishes in real-time.
>
> YOLO detects an elephant — bounding box drawn on the live feed, color-coded by threat level. Green for safe, cyan for detected threat, red for active alert.
>
> The 5-frame snapshot hits the backend. Now Amazon Nova 2 Lite takes over — in a single API call, it confirms the threat, classifies the behavior — charging versus grazing is a life-or-death distinction — scores severity one through ten, recommends a deterrent, and composes alerts in six languages. One call. Six outputs. Under two seconds.
>
> Amazon Nova Embed encodes the detection into a behavior vector and searches historical incidents for similar patterns — reducing false positives over time.

---

## [1:30–1:55] ALERTS + FEEDBACK

> Before five seconds are up, the farmer's phone buzzes.
>
> ntfy.sh push notification — works on any smartphone, no app required. Telegram message arrives with the detection photo, severity score, and two buttons: confirmed threat or false alarm.
>
> Every neighboring farm within fifteen kilometers gets a cascade alert automatically through the community mesh.
>
> When the farmer taps confirmed — that label goes straight into the training dataset. The model gets smarter. The next farm benefits.

---

## [1:55–2:20] NOVA AI ANALYSIS PANEL

> Here's what Nova returned. Behavior: elephant approaching perimeter fence. Severity: eight out of ten. Recommended deterrent: ultrasonic burst — fifteen to twenty-five kilohertz, inaudible to humans, proven effective against elephants.
>
> The threat meter visualizes severity. The voice alert is generated by Amazon Polly in the farmer's language — English, Hindi, Swahili, Spanish, Portuguese, or Arabic.
>
> Alert delivery status shows ntfy delivered, Telegram delivered, mesh broadcast sent, deterrent fired. Full observability on every channel.

---

## [2:20–2:40] INCIDENTS + DATASET

> The incidents tab shows every detection with severity badges, timestamps, and Nova's behavioral analysis.
>
> The dataset tab shows the auto-labeled training data — confirmed threats, false positives marked by farmers, and Nova-rejected low-confidence detections. This is the feedback loop: every farm that runs PAWS makes the model better for every farm that comes after.

---

## [2:40–3:00] CLOSING

> Five Amazon Nova models. Fourteen pipeline steps. Six languages. Zero dollars per alert.
>
> A farming cooperative with fifty members runs PAWS for roughly three-tenths of a cent per detection in Nova API costs. Electric fences start at eight hundred dollars.
>
> PAWS was never about a hackathon submission. It was about Bishnu sleeping through the night.
>
> This is PAWS — Perimeter AI Wildlife Surveillance. Built with Amazon Nova. #AmazonNova
